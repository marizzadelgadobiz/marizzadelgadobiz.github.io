Marizza Delgado

Marizza Delgado is a multifaceted Filipina American — an engineer, fashion model, and advocate for women in STEM. She earned her degree in Information Technology from the University of California, Santa Cruz and is currently pursuing a Master’s in Analytics with a focus on Computational Data at Georgia Tech. For nearly a decade, Marizza has modeled internationally for brands including H&M, L'Oréal, American Eagle, and Lululemon. She has walked in New York Fashion Week and appeared in Vogue Philippines. On June 30, 2024, she became the first Filipina to win Miss New York USA, later placing in the Top 20 at Miss USA. Her trailblazing work earned her recognition on the Forbes 30 Under 30 Class of 2025 in Arts & Style. Beyond the runway, Marizza is a data scientist with experience at Etsy and Rent the Runway, where she applied data to shape sustainable, customer-focused experiences. She also serves as a Council Leader on the Girls Who Code Advisory Council, helping empower the next generation of women in technology.

Appearances as Miss New York USA 2024 & Social Impact

As Miss New York USA 2024, Marizza Delgado has used her platform to inspire, advocate, and create meaningful change:

  • Public Speaking & Community Events: Featured speaker at Girls Who Code Summer Meetup Hosted by Morgan Stanley, One Girl Women in Charge Conference, Intrepid Museum Kids Week, and various university panels promoting diversity in tech and fashion.
  • Media Coverage: Interviewed by leading outlets including Vogue Philippines, Forbes, and PIX11, highlighting her journey as a Filipina American and STEM leader.
  • Philanthropy & Advocacy: Partnered with organizations such as New York Cares, Bowery Mission, and God's Love We Deliver for charitable campaigns supporting youth education, food insecurity, and environmental sustainability.
  • Cultural Representation: Advocated for increased visibility and representation of Filipina and Asian American women in fashion, media, and leadership roles.
  • Special Appearances: Speaker at the Forbes Women 30/50 Summit in Abu Dhabi, Host of the 2025 Fire Island Film Festival, and featured in the 2025 Philippines Independence Parade in NYC.

Beyond the crown, Marizza remains devoted to championing women in STEM and the arts, using her journey to inspire and open doors for future generations.
